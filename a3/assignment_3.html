How have video game sales changed for different platforms, genres, and publishers?:

It's clear that by looking at the visualizations, I can tell that the popularity of different genres and platforms change over time. While most genres maintain some relevance over the years, the sales of certain platforms decline, then drop over time as the technology becomes outdated and obsolete. There is no singular platform that has been and stayed popular forever, because new generations of it emerge and some become obsolete. For example, the PlayStation2 peaked sales in 2004, having the most sales of any plaform recorded. Over time, by 2010, global sales have declined from over 211 million to just 5.63 million. Looking at the data, it's interesting to see how the popularity of certain genres overlaps with certain platforms. For example, as shooter games peaked in 2011, PC and PS3 sales also peaked, leading me to believe that the release of shooter games, like Call of Duty 3 and Battlefield 3, being available on PC and PS3, lead to increased sales of these platforms.

Overall, both genre and platform sales are correlated with one another.
